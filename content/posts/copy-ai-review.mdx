## Key Features

### Brand Voice and Infobase

**Brand Voice** trains Copy.ai on your organization's writing style, tone, vocabulary, and brand personality. You provide examples of existing content, and Copy.ai learns the patterns — producing output that reflects your brand rather than generic AI writing.

**Infobase** stores factual context about your company, products, target customers, and competitive positioning. Rather than pasting product specs into every prompt, Infobase makes this information persistently available across all generated content.

Critically: **both Brand Voice and Infobase are available on the free plan**. This is not common in the category. Jasper's Brand Voice is a paid feature. Copy.ai's decision to make these core features freely accessible reflects genuine product generosity — and a smart acquisition strategy, since users who configure Brand Voice and Infobase invest time that creates switching cost.

For teams evaluating Copy.ai, this means you can fully test the brand consistency features before committing to any paid tier. The 2,000 word/month cap is the primary free plan limitation, not feature access.

### Multi-Model AI: GPT-4o + Claude

Copy.ai uses **multiple underlying AI models** — combining GPT-4o and Claude — rather than committing exclusively to one provider. Users can select models based on the task, and the platform internally routes certain requests to the model that performs best for that content type.

In practice, this architecture produces more consistent output than single-model tools. GPT-4o and Claude have different strengths — GPT-4o performs well on structured, factual content; Claude tends to produce more natural, nuanced prose. Routing between them based on task type produces better overall output than forcing all generation through one model.

The free plan accesses GPT-3.5 and Claude 3 Haiku — capable but not the most powerful available. Paid plans unlock GPT-4o and more capable Claude versions.

### Chat by Copy.ai

**Chat by Copy.ai** is the conversational interface — a ChatGPT-style interaction layer where you can brainstorm, iterate on drafts, ask for rewrites, and explore content directions in a dialogue format. It applies your Brand Voice and Infobase context automatically, so chat interactions stay on-brand without re-briefing.

For content workflows that involve significant back-and-forth iteration — refining a brief, exploring different angles on a topic, adjusting tone — Chat provides a more fluid interface than template-based generation. It's available on free and all paid plans.

### 90+ Templates

Copy.ai's **template library** covers the standard marketing content formats: blog post intros and outlines, Facebook and Google ad copy, email subject lines and sequences, social media captions, product descriptions, AIDA frameworks, PAS copy, landing page sections, and more.

The template library is where Copy.ai's 17 million user history shows. ## Workflow Automation: The Real Differentiator

**Workflow Automation** is the feature that most clearly separates Copy.ai from Jasper, Writesonic, and most other AI writing tools in 2026. It is also the feature that requires the most explanation — because "workflow automation" means something specific here, not just scheduling or batch generation.

A Copy.ai Workflow is a **multi-step automated content pipeline** that chains individual AI tasks together into a sequence that runs without manual intervention between steps. Each step in the workflow takes the output of the previous step as its input, applies AI processing, and passes the result forward.

An example workflow for a content marketing team:

1. **Input:** target keyword and product URL
2. **Step 1:** AI researches the topic via web search — competitor content, search intent, relevant data points
3. **Step 2:** AI generates a detailed outline based on the research, applying your Brand Voice
4. **Step 3:** AI drafts each section of the article using the outline and Infobase product context
5. **Step 4:** AI runs an SEO optimization pass — keyword integration, heading structure, meta description
6. **Output:** structured draft with research citations, ready for human editorial review

This sequence, without Workflow Automation, requires a human to manually complete each step, copy outputs between tools, and manage the context transfer. With a configured workflow, it runs on trigger — you provide the keyword, the pipeline does the rest.

**Sales outreach automation** is another primary use case: input a prospect's LinkedIn profile or company URL, the workflow researches the account, identifies relevant pain points, pulls context from your CRM, and generates a personalized outreach sequence — email 1, follow-up 2, follow-up 3 — tailored to the specific account. At scale, this type of personalization is impossible to do manually; with Copy.ai workflows, it runs on a list.

**The honest caveat:** Workflow Automation requires setup time. Building a reliable multi-step workflow — defining the steps, testing the outputs, handling edge cases, integrating with external systems — takes hours or days, not minutes. The productivity return is real, but it is not immediate. Teams that expect plug-and-play automation will be disappointed; teams willing to invest in configuration get significant ongoing leverage.

Workflow credits (usage units for automation runs) are included in paid plans, with higher tiers getting more credits. The free plan has very limited automation access — it's effectively a preview of the capability rather than a usable allocation.
