## Introduction

If you run an online business, a newsletter, or a side project that depends on email, you've probably felt the sting of Mailchimp's pricing as your list grows. You start free, you get comfortable, and then — boom — you hit 500 subscribers and suddenly you're paying $20/month just to keep the lights on.

That's exactly the pain point **Brevo** (formerly Sendinblue) was built to solve. Instead of charging you based on how many contacts you have, Brevo charges based on how many emails you send. For growing lists with moderate sending frequency, this model can save you hundreds of dollars a year.

But is Brevo actually good? Or is it just a cheaper alternative you'll eventually outgrow? I've been using Brevo for over a year across multiple projects, and this is my honest, no-fluff Brevo review for 2026.

---

## What Is Brevo?

Brevo is a French-founded (now globally operated) marketing platform that started life as Sendinblue back in 2012. It rebranded to Brevo in 2023 to reflect its expansion beyond just email into a full customer relationship suite.

Today, Brevo covers:

- **Email marketing** (newsletters, campaigns)
- **Marketing automation** (drip sequences, behavior triggers)
- **Transactional email** (order confirmations, password resets)
- **SMS marketing**
- **WhatsApp campaigns**
- **Live chat and chatbot**
- **CRM (contact management)**
- **Landing pages and signup forms**

For the average creator or small business owner, you likely care most about email marketing and automation — and that's where Brevo genuinely shines.

## Key Features

### Email Marketing

Brevo's email editor is clean, drag-and-drop, and gets out of your way. You can start from one of 40+ templates or build from scratch. The rendering is consistent across Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail — something that's surprisingly hard to get right.

One thing I appreciate: **the free plan includes all templates and the full editor**. No paywalled templates trying to upsell you mid-campaign.

### Marketing Automation

This is where Brevo punches above its weight. Even on the free plan, you get access to automation workflows — something competitors like Mailchimp only unlock on paid tiers.

You can build sequences triggered by:
- Email opens or clicks
- Page visits (with their tracker installed)
- Form submissions
- Specific dates (birthday emails, anniversary sequences)
- Custom events via API

The visual workflow builder isn't as polished as ActiveCampaign's, but it's functional and covers 90% of what most small businesses need.

### The Free Plan

Let's be direct about the **Brevo free plan**, because it's one of the most frequently searched topics:

- **300 emails/day** (9,000/month)
- **Unlimited contacts** — this is the killer feature
- Access to automation, templates, and the API
- Brevo badge on emails (removable on paid plans)

The unlimited contacts policy is genuinely rare. Most competitors cap you at 500–2,000 contacts before charging. With Brevo, you can import 50,000 subscribers and still send on the free plan — as long as you stay under 300 emails/day.

For a newsletter with 5,000 subscribers sending once a week? You'd send all 5,000 in batches over a couple of days. Manageable, but not ideal for time-sensitive campaigns.

### Transactional Email

If you run a SaaS or e-commerce store, you'll love Brevo's transactional email capabilities. You get a generous free tier for transactional sends (separate from marketing) and solid deliverability backed by their own sending infrastructure.

Their SMTP integration is straightforward, and they offer SDKs for Node.js, PHP, Python, and more — which is why we actually use their API in this very site for the newsletter signup form.

### Contact Management and Segmentation

Brevo's CRM is basic but functional. You can segment contacts by custom attributes, engagement history (opens, clicks), geographic location, and more. Dynamic lists update automatically as contacts meet or exit criteria.

For advanced segmentation with complex boolean logic, you'll want something like ActiveCampaign. But for standard list hygiene and targeted campaigns, Brevo's tools are more than enough.

## Pricing and Plans (2026)

Brevo's pricing model is genuinely different from most competitors. Here's how it breaks down:

| Plan | Price | Monthly Emails | Key Features |
|------|-------|----------------|--------------|
| **Free** | $0 | 9,000 (300/day) | Unlimited contacts, automation, templates |
| **Starter** | From $9/mo | 5,000+ | No daily limit, remove Brevo branding |
| **Business** | From $18/mo | 5,000+ | A/B testing, advanced reporting, landing pages |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Custom | Dedicated IP, SSO, phone support |

The Starter plan starts at $9/month for 5,000 emails and scales based on volume. At 100,000 emails/month, you're looking at around $65/month — compare that to Mailchimp's $100+/month for a list of 10,000 contacts.

**Important for 2026:** Brevo has held its pricing relatively stable compared to competitors who raised rates significantly in 2024–2025. That price stability is a meaningful differentiator.

## Brevo vs Mailchimp: The Real Comparison

Since "Brevo vs Mailchimp" is one of the top questions I get, here's my honest take:

**Choose Brevo if:**
- Your list is large but you don't send every day
- You want powerful automation without paying $50+/month
- You need transactional email in the same platform
- You're budget-conscious and value the free plan

**Choose Mailchimp if:**
- You need best-in-class reporting and analytics
- Your team is already trained on Mailchimp
- You use a lot of third-party integrations (Mailchimp has more)
- Deliverability on high-volume sends is critical (Mailchimp's reputation is marginally stronger)

For most solo creators and small businesses, **Brevo wins on value**. Mailchimp wins on ecosystem breadth.

## Who Is Brevo For?

After a year of using it, here's my honest assessment of who benefits most from Brevo:

**Perfect fit:**
- **Newsletter creators** with growing lists (5K–100K subscribers)
- **Affiliates and bloggers** who want to build an email list without paying per contact
- **Small e-commerce stores** that need both marketing and transactional email
- **SaaS founders** who want a single API for all email types
- **Agencies** managing multiple client lists under one account

**Less ideal:**
- Enterprise teams needing advanced CRM integration
- High-volume senders (500K+ emails/month) where dedicated ESPs may perform better
- Teams heavily invested in the Meta or Google ads ecosystem (Mailchimp integrates better)

## Final Verdict

After using Brevo across multiple projects, I'd rate it **4 out of 5 stars**.

It's not perfect — the daily limit on the free plan is annoying, and the interface occasionally feels like it was designed by engineers rather than UX designers. But the **value proposition is unbeatable** for the target audience.

The contact-based vs email-volume pricing model alone can save small business owners $500–2,000/year compared to Mailchimp or ConvertKit. Add in free automation, a solid transactional email API, and a genuinely useful free plan, and Brevo earns its spot as the **best value email marketing platform in 2026**.

If you're looking for an honest answer to "is Brevo worth it" — yes, it is. Especially if you're just starting out or running a tight budget.
