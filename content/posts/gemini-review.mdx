## What Is Google Gemini?

Google Gemini is the AI assistant (and underlying model family) built by **Google DeepMind**, Google's consolidated AI research division. Gemini replaced Google Bard in February 2024 and has since become the core AI layer across Google's entire product portfolio.

Gemini is available across:
- **Gemini.google.com** — standalone web interface
- **Google Workspace** — integrated into Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Slides, Drive
- **Android** — as the default assistant on most Android devices
- **Google Search** — AI Mode uses Gemini for intelligent search responses

The **flagship model as of May 2026** is **Gemini 3.1 Pro**, which powers the Pro tier and above. The free tier runs on **Gemini 3 Flash** — a fast, cost-efficient model — with limited access to Gemini 3.1 Pro. The cheapest API option is **Gemini 2.5 Flash-Lite**, designed for high-volume, budget-sensitive developer use.

What sets Gemini apart from Claude and ChatGPT is not any single feature — it's the degree to which it's embedded in Google's infrastructure. If your work lives in Gmail and Google Docs, Gemini doesn't feel like a third-party add-on. It feels like a native capability of tools you already use every day.

## Key Features

### 1. Native Google Workspace Integration

This is Gemini's defining advantage. Gemini integrates directly into:

- **Gmail** — drafts emails, summarizes long threads, extracts action items, translates messages
- **Google Docs** — rewrites sections, adjusts tone, generates outlines, expands bullet points into paragraphs
- **Google Sheets** — writes formulas, analyzes data, generates chart recommendations, fills columns with AI-generated content
- **Google Slides** — creates full presentations from a text prompt, suggests design improvements

For teams that already live in Google Workspace, this level of integration is genuinely transformative. You don't need to copy-paste between an AI assistant and your work tools — Gemini is already inside the document you're editing.

No other AI assistant at this price point offers this depth of Workspace integration. Microsoft Copilot does something similar for Microsoft 365, but Gemini's implementation is more polished as of May 2026.

### 2. Deep Research

Gemini's **Deep Research** is an autonomous research agent that browses hundreds of websites, evaluates sources, and synthesizes findings into a structured report with citations. Unlike a standard AI response drawn from training data, Deep Research actively explores the current web in real time.

- **Free plan:** 5 Deep Research reports per month
- **Pro plan:** 20 Deep Research sessions per day

In our testing, Gemini's Deep Research consistently produced more thorough, better-cited reports than ChatGPT's equivalent feature. The agent's ability to identify conflicting information across sources and flag it explicitly is particularly valuable for research-heavy professionals.

### 3. Veo 3.1 — Video Generation

**Veo 3.1** is Google's AI video generation model, available on the **Pro plan ($19.99/month) and above**. Veo 3.1 generates short video clips from text descriptions and can animate still images into motion sequences.

This is a direct competitor to OpenAI's Sora. As of May 2026, Veo 3.1 produces noticeably more realistic motion physics and better temporal consistency across frames than earlier Veo versions. For social media creators, marketers, and educators producing visual content, access to Veo 3.1 at the Pro price point is exceptional value — ChatGPT charges $20/month for Sora access, while Gemini Pro includes Veo 3.1 at essentially the same price.

### 4. NotebookLM — Document Analysis

**NotebookLM** is one of Google's most distinctive AI products. It lets you upload documents, PDFs, research papers, and other text sources, and then ask questions, generate summaries, create study guides, and produce audio overviews directly from your own source material.

Unlike asking a general AI assistant to summarize a document, NotebookLM anchors every response to the specific sources you've provided — and cites exactly where each claim comes from. For researchers, students, lawyers, and analysts working with large document collections, this grounding in verified sources reduces hallucination risk significantly.

The expanded NotebookLM capabilities are included from the **AI Plus plan** ($7.99/month) onwards.

### 5. Gemini Live — Real-Time Voice

**Gemini Live** enables natural, real-time voice conversation with Gemini — available on Android and iOS. The latency is low, the voice quality is natural, and you can interrupt mid-sentence as you would in a real conversation.

On the free tier, Gemini Live is available with some usage limits. Paid tiers unlock extended sessions and higher-quality voice models. For commuters, accessibility users, and anyone who prefers spoken interaction over typing, Gemini Live is competitive with ChatGPT's Advanced Voice Mode.

### 6. Gemini Code Assist

**Gemini Code Assist** is Google's IDE-integrated coding assistant, available from the Pro plan. It supports VS Code, JetBrains IDEs, and other major development environments.

Code Assist handles autocomplete, function generation, bug explanation, and test writing directly in the editor. For developers already using Google Cloud or Firebase, Code Assist integrates particularly well with Google's backend services.

That said, for complex autonomous coding tasks — multi-file refactors, architectural decisions, end-to-end feature implementation — Claude Code remains more capable. Gemini Code Assist is a strong IDE companion, not a replacement for agentic coding workflows.

### 7. Gems — Custom AI Personas

**Gems** are Gemini's version of ChatGPT's Custom GPTs. You can create specialized AI personas with custom instructions, specific knowledge, and defined communication styles — then reuse them across conversations.

Practical examples: a Gem configured as a legal brief editor that always references specific citation formats, a customer support Gem trained on your product documentation, or a research Gem that always structures outputs in a specific academic format.
